Engagements may involve software, firmware, hardware, web systems, mobile applications, Bitcoin infrastructure, artificial intelligence, machine learning, OSINT, cyber investigation, cybersecurity, cyber-warfare response, creative technology, or advanced research and development.

Work is performed through private, scoped engagements with clearly defined objectives, boundaries, deliverables, assumptions, and payment terms.

Engagement Structure

How Consulting Engagements Work

Engagements are structured to reduce ambiguity, protect both parties, and establish a clear technical and operational basis before work begins.

01

Initial Contact

The client provides a concise description of the problem, objective, urgency, constraints, and expected outcome.

02

Qualification

The request is reviewed for technical fit, lawful purpose, scope, risk, required access, and available capacity.

03

Scope & Terms

Deliverables, exclusions, assumptions, communications, confidentiality, timeline, payment, and responsibilities are defined in writing.

04

Execution

Work proceeds according to the agreed scope, with progress, findings, risks, and decisions documented as appropriate.

Important: No engagement begins solely from an unsolicited message. Work begins only after the request has been accepted, the scope and terms have been agreed, and the required payment or retainer has been confirmed.
