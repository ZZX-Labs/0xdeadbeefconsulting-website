Project Lifecycle

From Concept to Maintained System

Projects may progress through multiple stages and may return to earlier stages as requirements, research, hardware, software, or operational conditions change.

01

Concept

Problem definition, objectives, users, use cases, constraints, assumptions, and initial technical direction.

02

Research

Prior art, feasibility, standards, dependencies, risks, architecture, security, and implementation options.

03

Prototype

Proof-of-concept implementation, experiments, testing, instrumentation, failure analysis, and refinement.

04

Development

Full implementation, integration, documentation, testing, packaging, deployment, and operational planning.

05

Validation

Security review, performance analysis, compatibility, usability, reliability, and acceptance testing.

06

Release

Public or private delivery, deployment, source release, documentation, licensing, and versioning.

07

Operation

Monitoring, maintenance, support, backups, incident response, upgrades, and long-term use.

08

Revision

Lessons learned, technical debt, new requirements, redesign, migration, expansion, or retirement.
