Professional Relationship

Contact Does Not Automatically Create an Engagement

A consultant-client relationship exists only after explicit written acceptance and agreement upon the applicable scope, responsibilities, payment, confidentiality, and other terms.

Actions That Do Not Create an Engagement

  • Visiting or using this website.
  • Sending an email or contact request.
  • Submitting a proposal, NDA, résumé, or project archive.
  • Sending unsolicited technical information.
  • Requesting a quotation or estimate.
  • Making a Bitcoin donation.
  • Following or messaging a public social-media account.
  • Receiving a preliminary response or general information.

Requirements for an Accepted Engagement

  • Explicit written acceptance.
  • Defined parties and authorized representatives.
  • Lawful purpose and authorized access.
  • Agreed scope and deliverables.
  • Documented exclusions and assumptions.
  • Payment or retainer confirmation.
  • Applicable NDA or confidentiality terms.
  • Required engagement, waiver, or service documents.
Do not send sensitive information before acceptance: Avoid sending credentials, private keys, seed phrases, customer data, protected records, malware, exploit code, confidential evidence, classified information, or restricted technical data unless secure handling has been expressly arranged.
