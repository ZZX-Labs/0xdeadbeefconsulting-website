Engagement Process

From Inquiry to Delivery

Work is structured to establish lawful purpose, technical fit, clear expectations, defined responsibilities, and secure operating conditions before implementation begins.

01

Inquiry

The client provides a concise description of the problem, desired result, urgency, environment, constraints, and confidentiality requirements.

02

Qualification

The request is reviewed for lawful purpose, technical fit, authorization, risk, required access, schedule, and available capacity.

03

Discovery

Requirements, stakeholders, architecture, dependencies, evidence, documentation, operational conditions, and expected outcomes are examined.

04

Scope

Deliverables, exclusions, assumptions, milestones, access, communications, confidentiality, acceptance, and payment terms are documented.

05

Payment

The required Bitcoin payment or retainer is issued, verified, transmitted, and confirmed before scheduled work begins.

06

Execution

Research, engineering, development, testing, investigation, reporting, or advisory work proceeds according to scope.

07

Validation

Results are reviewed against requirements, security, quality, performance, compatibility, and acceptance conditions.

08

Delivery

Deliverables, documentation, findings, limitations, recommendations, credentials, source, or artifacts are transferred as agreed.

No work from unsolicited submission: Sending files, source code, credentials, project archives, documents, evidence, an NDA, or a payment request does not create an engagement or authorize work.

Professional Boundaries

Lawful, Authorized, and Clearly Scoped Work

Requests are evaluated for lawful purpose, client authority, professional suitability, technical risk, and alignment with ethical and operational boundaries.

Required Conditions

  • Lawful purpose.
  • Authorized access.
  • Accurate representation of the client and project.
  • Defined scope and responsibilities.
  • Secure handling for sensitive material.
  • Applicable confidentiality terms.
  • Agreed payment terms.
  • Compliance with relevant law and regulation.

Requests That May Be Declined

  • Unauthorized access or surveillance.
  • Fraud, theft, extortion, harassment, or abuse.
  • Malware deployment or destructive intrusion.
  • Credential theft or private-key extraction.
  • Illegal data acquisition or disclosure.
  • Requests involving misrepresentation or impersonation.
  • Work prohibited by sanctions or export controls.
  • Projects exceeding available capability or capacity.
Security work requires authorization: No cybersecurity, OSINT, investigative, forensic, monitoring, testing, or response work should be assumed authorized without documented permission and a clearly defined scope.
