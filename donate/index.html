Address Verification

Verify Before You Send

Bitcoin transactions are generally irreversible. Address verification should be treated as a required security step, not an optional precaution.

01

Compare the Address

Compare every character of the copied address with the address displayed on the official website.

02

Check the Destination

Confirm that the wallet displays the expected address before approving or signing the transaction.

03

Use a Test Transaction

For larger donations, consider sending a smaller test transaction before transmitting the intended amount.

04

Confirm Independently

When uncertain, verify the address through an independent, trusted communication channel before sending Bitcoin.

Clipboard malware warning: Malicious software may replace a copied Bitcoin address with an attacker's address. Always compare the complete destination after pasting it into your wallet.
Donation Policy

Voluntary, Unrestricted Support

A donation is a voluntary transfer of Bitcoin and does not purchase consulting services, create a professional relationship, reserve future availability, or guarantee a particular project outcome.

What a Donation Does

  • Supports independent technical work.
  • Helps fund public research and documentation.
  • Supports open-source software and infrastructure.
  • Contributes to experimentation and prototype development.
  • Helps preserve long-term project independence.

What a Donation Does Not Do

  • Create a consultant-client relationship.
  • Purchase services, deliverables, or support hours.
  • Guarantee project completion or publication.
  • Create ownership, equity, voting, or governance rights.
  • Provide priority access to consulting availability.
  • Replace a written consulting agreement or invoice.
No restricted-purpose assumption: Unless a separate written agreement expressly states otherwise, donations are treated as voluntary general support and may be used wherever they are most useful to the broader work.

Privacy

Public Blockchain Considerations

Bitcoin transactions are recorded on a public ledger. Donations should not be treated as inherently anonymous.

Public Transactions

Transaction amounts, inputs, outputs, timestamps, and address activity may be visible through public blockchain explorers and analysis tools.

Address Reuse

Reusing public addresses can increase transaction linkability and reveal additional information about wallet activity.

Donor Identity

Blockchain data may be associated with identity through exchanges, payment services, communications, network metadata, or other external records.
